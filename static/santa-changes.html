Secret Santa — recent changes

Summary of fixes made after the first round, 2026-05-26

Here is everything that was changed based on your feedback. For each item I've added a Limitation note — that's what the change does not do, so you can tell me if you want to push it further.

1 The post-draw email no longer says "Secret Santa" in the subject

The reveal email's subject line was the only place still mentioning "Secret Santa" by name. It is now generic and uses only the event title.

Before
FRVotre tirage Secret Santa pour Noël 2026
ENYour Secret Santa draw for Christmas 2026
After
FRVotre tirage pour Noël 2026
ENYour draw for Christmas 2026
Limitation The subject now relies entirely on the event title set by the admin. If next year's event is called "December 2026" the subject will be "Your draw for December 2026". If you want a warmer wording (e.g. "Your gift assignment for…"), tell me and I'll change it.

2 New "How it works" block at the top of the sign-up page

A numbered card now appears above the registration form, so people see the flow before reading anything else. Goal: prevent the "I'll just go buy three presents right away" reaction. This is exactly what participants will see at the top of the page:

English version
How it works
  1. Sign up with your name and email.
  2. You'll receive a personal link by email to fill in your wish list.
  3. On draw day, you'll receive by email the name of just one person to give a gift to.
Version française
Comment ça marche
  1. Inscrivez-vous avec votre nom et votre email.
  2. Recevez par email un lien personnel pour remplir votre liste de souhaits.
  3. Le jour du tirage, vous recevrez par email le nom d'une seule personne à qui offrir un cadeau.
Limitation The block sits after the event description that the admin types in. If that description is long, the "How it works" box and the sign-up button still get pushed down. Easy workaround for next year: keep the event description very short (one or two sentences) and let the "How it works" box do the explaining.

3 Sign-up button is now closer to the top of the form

You asked for the button to be at the top. The disclaimer paragraph ("We cannot guarantee… exercise in GIVING, not receiving") was sitting inside the form, between the title and the name fields, which pushed the button far down. It has been moved below the button, smaller, so the action is the focus.

  3. ⚠️ Long yellow disclaimer
  4. Name fields
  5. Email field
  6. Sign-up button
After — order on the page
  1. "Secret Santa Registration" title
  2. Short intro line

  5. Sign-up button
  6. ⚠️ Disclaimer (moved below)
Limitation The disclaimer text itself was not shortened or reworded — it still says "this is an exercise in GIVING, not receiving". If you want a shorter or warmer version, I can edit it — but the same text is also used inside the first email (the one with the personal link), so any change there will appear in both places.

4 Clarifying sentence at the top of the wish-list form

Several people apparently read the three-wish form as "I have to buy these three things". A clarifying sentence has been added right above the wish fields so the point of view is explicit: these are wishes for them, not a shopping list by them. Below is the real form, before and after, in the French version (English gets the same sentence in English).

Before
Ma liste de souhaits
Pour ceux qui n'ont pas le temps, quelque chose qui peut être acheté (moins de 10 €) *
Un stylo, des chaussettes, du chocolat…

Enregistrer ma liste
After
Ma liste de souhaits

Ce sont les souhaits que verra la personne qui vous offrira un cadeau. Vous, vous n'offrirez qu'un cadeau, à la personne qui vous sera attribuée au moment du tirage.

Pour ceux qui n'ont pas le temps, quelque chose qui peut être acheté (moins de 10 €) *
Un stylo, des chaussettes, du chocolat…
Pour ceux qui n'ont pas d'argent, quelque chose qui peut être fabriqué ou trouvé *
Une plante, un plat, un poème, une prière…

Enregistrer ma liste

The English version of the form receives the same sentence inserted in the same position: "These are the wishes the person giving you a gift will see. You will only give one gift yourself — to the person assigned to you at the draw."

Limitation The labels of the three wish fields themselves were not rewritten. They still read like instructions to the giver ("For those short on time, something that can be bought…"). If you want the labels also reworded into plain wish-list framing (e.g. "Idea 1: something to buy under €10", "Idea 2: something to make or find", "Idea 3: anything you like"), tell me and I'll change them.

If any of this should be tweaked, or if you want to go further on a particular point, just tell me what you'd like and I'll apply it. — Jérémy
